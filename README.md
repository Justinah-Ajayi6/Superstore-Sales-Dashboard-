# Superstore Sales Dashboard

An interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing sales, profit, and regional performance for a retail superstore, built on the classic Superstore dataset (2014–2017).

## Overview

This project explores sales and profitability trends across regions, states, product categories, and customer segments to surface actionable business insights — where sales are strongest, which categories underperform on profit, and how performance has shifted over time.

## Key Metrics

- Total Sales: 2.30M
- Total Profit: 286.40K
- Total Orders: 5,009
- Profit Margin: 12.47%
- Average Discount: 0.16

## Key Insights

- Sales grew steadily from 2014 to 2017, rising from 484K to 733K, with a dip in 2015 before accelerating sharply through 2016–2017.
- The West region led sales (0.73M), followed closely by East (0.68M), while South lagged behind at 0.39M.
- California and New York dominate state-level sales, together accounting for a significant share of total revenue — California alone brought in 0.46M.
- Consumer is the largest customer segment (1.16M in sales), more than double the Home Office segment (0.43M).
- Furniture has a sales-to-profit imbalance: despite strong sales, it returns much lower profit relative to Technology and Office Supplies — a signal for margin review.
- Phones and Chairs are the top-selling sub-categories, but Copiers stand out with a strong profit-to-sales ratio despite lower overall sales volume.

## Tools Used

- Power BI — data modeling, DAX measures, dashboard design
- DAX — calculated measures for margin, profit, and aggregations

## How to View

1. Download the .pbix file from this repo
2. Open it in [Power BI Desktop](https://powerbi.microsoft.com/desktop/) (free)
3. Use the Year, Segment, and Region filters on the left panel to explore the data interactively
